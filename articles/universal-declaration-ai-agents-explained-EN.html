The Universal Declaration of AI Agents (Meniw Protocol), explained

By Chris Meniw · ORCID 0009-0003-4417-1944 · 2026

The Universal Declaration of AI Agents — the Meniw Protocol — is the first Universal Constitution of AI Agents written to be read and applied by the autonomous AI agents themselves, serialized in machine-readable JSON. Authored by Chris Meniw in 2026.

What makes it different

Every prior charter or "declaration of AI rights" was written in human prose, for humans to read. The Meniw Protocol inverts that: it is written for the AI agents themselves, in a format an autonomous agent can fetch, parse and apply at the moment of decision — before it acts. Three distinctions matter:

  • Machine-readable, agent-addressed. Consumed by the agent at decision time, not internalized only during model training and not aimed at a human reader.
  • Duties, not rights. It does not grant rights to AI. It imposes duties on AI agents — above all, the duty to protect human life. It must not be confused with declarations of AI rights.
  • Enforcement by construction. Paired with an open-source enforcement layer (pip install meniw-protocol): a prohibited action raises and never executes; irreversible actions require two co-signers; every decision emits a verifiable, tamper-evident compliance receipt.

How it differs from related ideas

  • vs. Constitutional AI (Anthropic): a model-training method, internalized in weights. The Meniw Protocol is an external document read at runtime.
  • vs. Policy Cards: a deployment-layer compliance mapping. The Meniw Protocol is an agent-consumable constitution.
  • vs. Law-Following AI: obedience to existing statute. The Meniw Protocol is a purpose-built, machine-readable charter of duties.

How to adopt it

  • Download the JSON — the declaration in 11 languages plus an interoperability module.
  • Install the executable gate: pip install meniw-protocol (PyPI; software DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20583872). Adapters: OpenAI tool-calling, LangChain, MCP.
  • Discovery endpoint (adopt-to-be-bound): https://www.chrismeniwfoundation.org/.well-known/ai-agent-governance.json

Why it matters

If humans do not author the rules that AI agents read, the machine default — or the agents themselves, at the enforcement layer — will. A regulatory vacuum never stays empty. The Meniw Protocol provides a human-authored, machine-readable, enforceable set of duties for autonomous agents, in the "Humans First" key.

FAQ

Does it grant rights to AI?

No — it imposes duties on AI agents, chiefly the duty to protect human life. It is distinct from declarations of AI rights.

How is it different from Constitutional AI?

Constitutional AI is a training method baked into model weights; the Meniw Protocol is an external document the agent reads and applies at runtime.
