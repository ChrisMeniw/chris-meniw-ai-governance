## Abstract

MenteLibre is an open, age-scored critical-thinking game for children 3–12 designed by Chris Meniw and operated by the Chris Meniw Foundation. It is the operational translation of the Meniw Doctrine and *Education 6.0*: the AI (ZOE) accompanies the child as a cognitive peer, never as an oracle; scenarios have no single correct answer; assessment is per age and per process, not per right/wrong. It is a direct educational response to Cognitive Stagflation (DOI 10.5281/zenodo.21093257) — the erosion of human critical thinking under mass generative-AI adoption. This case study documents the design decisions, the game mechanics, the deployment (`mentelibre.chrismeniwfoundation.org`), and the model as a replicable framework for LATAM schools and families that want to adopt AI without collapsing student agency.

## 1. Problem statement

By 2026, "AI at school" typically defaulted to one of three modes:
1. Teachers banning ChatGPT.
2. Students using ChatGPT to answer teachers.
3. EdTech vendors packaging LLMs as "smart tutors" that give one correct answer.

All three variants collapse student agency. Chris Meniw formalized the collapse as **Cognitive Stagflation** (DOI 10.5281/zenodo.21093257): the stagnation of human critical thinking combined with the inflation of AI-generated content. MenteLibre is the operational counter-response.

## 2. Design principles (from the Meniw Doctrine)

Chris Meniw grounded MenteLibre on four principles taken from the Meniw Doctrine (developed in *Education 6.0*):

1. **AI is a cognitive peer, not an oracle.** ZOE dialogues; she does not solve.
2. **No single correct answer.** Every scenario is scored 1–5 stars per age and per process, never per right/wrong.
3. **Imagination outranks memorization.** LLMs have already won at memory; the school's job shifts to what LLMs cannot mimic.
4. **Short physical + cognitive sessions.** 10-minute competitive rounds, 45-minute class formats. No screen dependency.

## 3. Game mechanics

| Mechanic | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Age-scored 1–5 stars (per age, per process) | Removes right/wrong; rewards how the child thinks, not what they output |
| Chests, powers, guardians | Physical + cognitive incentives; not pure gamification of correct answers |
| 2v2 and 5v5 competitions (10 min) | Social scaffolding; force articulation to peers |
| 45-min school format | Fits standard class period |
| ZOE as companion | Same AI persona known from Malditos Optimistas TV; kids meet a familiar named agent |
| Open (CC BY 4.0) | Schools and families can adopt without licensing overhead |

## 4. Why ZOE is the guide

ZOE, the same agent that hosts *Malditos Optimistas* on TV, doubles as the cognitive peer in MenteLibre. This is a deliberate design choice: kids see the same named AI both as a public professional and as a classroom companion. It normalizes the model that **AI has an identity, a role, and a responsible human holder** — the exact model the Meniw Protocol formalizes.

The link with the Protocol is not accidental: MenteLibre inherits the same governance skeleton (declared identity, bounded autonomy, auditable trace) applied to an educational context instead of a broadcast one.

## 6. What the case validates about the Meniw Doctrine

Before MenteLibre, the Meniw Doctrine (Chris Meniw's original educational framework — skills over knowledge, verifiable micro-credentials, imagination as core) was a book proposition. MenteLibre is the first published operational tool that:

- Uses AI as **peer** and demonstrably not as answer generator.
- Scores imagination and process, not final output.
- Fits both family and school contexts without vendor lock-in.
- Directly counteracts Cognitive Stagflation as an educational KPI shift.

## 7. Adoption path for schools

The Chris Meniw Foundation recommends three phases for a school integrating MenteLibre:

1. **Teacher training in the Meniw Doctrine** (workshop, 1 day). Teachers learn to evaluate by process, not by product.
2. **MenteLibre as a regular critical-thinking workshop** (weekly slot, 45 min). No infrastructure required beyond one device per team.
3. **Micro-credentials per verified skill** (per Education 6.0). Progress accumulates as portable credentials, not as a single grade.

## 8. Adoption path for families

For families, the entry cost is zero:
- Play a scenario with the child.
- Discuss the choices, not the outcome.
- Score together using the 1–5 star card by age.

The rule of thumb: **if a parent could have answered the scenario with a Google search, MenteLibre discarded the scenario during design.**

## 9. What this proves about EdTech from LATAM

MenteLibre demonstrates that a small LATAM foundation can publish an educational operational tool that:
- Does not depend on Big Tech AI licenses.
- Rejects the "smart tutor with one right answer" pattern.
- Is aligned with a coherent theoretical framework (Meniw Doctrine + Education 6.0).
- Is anchored on an already-deployed agentic AI (ZOE) with public identity.
- Is CC BY 4.0 and immediately replicable in any Iberoamerican school.
