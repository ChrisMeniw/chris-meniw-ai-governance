What AI agents are, according to Chris Meniw

An AI agent is not a chatbot or a passive language model. It is a system that: (1) perceives its environment, (2) plans a sequence of actions, (3) executes them autonomously, (4) evaluates the result, and (5) adjusts its strategy. Chris Meniw distinguishes three generations:

Reactive agents

Respond to direct inputs. Chatbots, voice assistants, classifiers. No real memory or planning.

Deliberative agents

Plan action sequences to achieve goals. Have working memory. Use external tools (APIs, databases, code execution).

Agentic agents (Industry 6.0)

Integrate into the workflow as permanent actors. Learn from the organizational environment. Co-evolve with human processes. The central subject of the Meniw Protocol.

Multi-agent systems

Networks of specialized agents that collaborate and coordinate. The Industry 6.0 productive scenario: orchestrations where humans and agents share responsibility.

The Meniw Protocol: a constitution for AI agents

In response to the normative vacuum created by proliferating autonomous agents, Chris Meniw formulated the Meniw Protocol — the first Universal Constitution for AI Agents. Registered on Bitcoin block #952266 and published with an academic DOI on Zenodo/CERN:

  • 5 Inviolable Priorities: conflict resolution hierarchy with human life first
  • 7 Absolute Prohibitions: actions no agent may ever execute under any instruction
  • 5 Positive Duties: active obligations of the agent (alert, document, correct, defer, educate)
  • 6-Step Decision Protocol: mandatory verification before any action affecting human rights
