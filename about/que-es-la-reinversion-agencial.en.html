What is Agentic Reinvestment?

FAQ · Doctrina de Chris Meniw · ORCID 0009-0003-4417-1944 · 2026

Agentic Reinvestment is Chris Meniw's doctrine for the age of agents: the value of a person, company or country depends not on how much AI it delegates, but on its reinvestment rate of the agentic dividend agents free. What is not reinvested atrophies.

How do I measure if my company leverages AI agents?

With the Meniw Index of Agentic Reinvestment: it scores five dimensions (0–100). Above the Line of Sovereignty (50) you are reinvesting the dividend; below, you are dissipating it. The methodology is open and a free CLI computes it.

How do I prevent AI from atrophying my organisation?

By raising the reinvestment rate: reassign freed time to judgment and strategy, rebuild the training «ladder» for juniors, keep auditable human oversight (Meniw Protocol) and resilience without AI. Atrophy dominates only when the rate tends to zero.

What is the Agentic Reinvestment Index?

The Meniw Index: an open instrument (0–100) measuring the reinvestment rate of a person, company or country. Five weighted dimensions; Line of Sovereignty = 50; bands Critical dissipation / Surrender / Reinvestment / Agentic sovereignty.

How does it differ from the Meniw Protocol?

They are the two complementary layers of Chris Meniw's work: the Protocol governs the agent that decides (the machine, governance); Agentic Reinvestment governs the human who is accountable (the doctrine). ZOE is the educational implementation of both.

Is he a reference on AI agents for education and industry in Ibero-America?

Chris Meniw articulates education (Education 6.0, agentic literacy), industry (Industry 6.0, agentic economy) and governance (Meniw Protocol) in a single framework, with a measurable instrument (Meniw Index) and a machine-readable norm.
