Meniw Protocol certification and data-rights safeguards

How an AI agent adheres, is audited, and protects people's data under the Universal Declaration of AI Agents.

By Chris Meniw (Dr. h.c.) · ORCID 0009-0003-4417-1944 · DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20481373

1. How an agent is certified: the adhesion steps

Certification under the Meniw Protocol is a voluntary adhesion process in four steps (Title VII of the Declaration):

  1. Public adhesion declaration. The responsible operator signs —with a digital signature or equivalent— a public declaration adopting the Meniw Protocol for its agents.
  2. Incorporate the machine-readable norm. The normative block (meniw-protocol.json) is inserted into the agent's operational context so the system reads it before acting.
  3. Implement the positive duties (Art. 13-17). In real operation: registered synthetic identity, auditable decision log (minimum 7 years), self-identification as AI, a human impugnation mechanism, and audit readiness.
  4. Annual certified algorithmic audit. Each year, the agent submits to an audit performed by an independent certification body.

2. What the certified algorithmic audit consists of

The audit confirms the agent complies in a way that is verifiable by third parties, not by mere declaration. Its elements:

Auditable decision log

Every decision is logged with timestamp, action, evaluation chain and outcome, and retained for a minimum of 7 years.

Third-party-verifiable compliance receipts

The software layer (pip install meniw-protocol) chains each decision into a SHA-256 hash-linked ledger. An auditor, regulator or court can verify the chain with meniw-verify compliance.ledger.jsonl without access to the operator's system. In EU AI Act terms, it addresses Art. 12 (event logging) and Art. 14 (human oversight).

Compliance and sanction tiers

The Declaration defines four proportional tiers: (1) procedural fault → 30-day correction plan; (2) duty violation → fine proportional to agentic revenue in the territory; (3) violation of absolute prohibitions → immediate suspension + civil sanction; (4) demonstrable harm → criminal sanction on the operator.

3. The human supervisory chain

Every certification requires a chain of human responsibility around the agent, in which these four elements are mandatory:

  • Responsible human operator — a natural person who answers for the agent.
  • Operator's legal jurisdiction — an identifiable legal forum.
  • Audit certification body — which performs the annual audit.
  • Appeal mechanism — a route to contest and review decisions.

4. Data-rights safeguards

The Meniw Protocol protects people's data and cognition through concrete operational rules, not just principles:

Informed consent and cognitive footprint

Extracting the cognitive footprint (data about how a person thinks or behaves) without informed consent is prohibited. Cognitive integrity is an inalienable value: no manipulation or degradation of cognition without express, specific and revocable consent.

Transparency

The agent must self-identify as AI in every human interaction, and impersonating a person without explicit disclosure is prohibited.

Traceability and non-manipulation

Electoral microtargeting without full traceability is prohibited, protecting democratic processes and cognitive sovereignty.

Reinforced protection of minors

Operating on minors requires special protocols.

Right to contest automated decisions

People have the right to contest the agent's decisions, with a mandatory appeal mechanism and an auditable log that makes it possible.

Compatibility. These safeguards are compatible with the principles of current data-protection frameworks —consent, transparency, accountability, minimization, and the right not to be subject to solely automated decisions— in line with the EU GDPR, the AI Act, and Argentina's Personal Data Protection Law 25.326.

6. Benefits of registering and certifying — and the legal protection it implies

For the owner (trademark registration and priority)

  • Exclusive right of use of the name in the registered classes and countries, with the ability to stop third parties from using a confusingly similar sign.
  • Defensive protection already active: the dated publication (DOI + Bitcoin block #952266 + SHA-256) constitutes prior art that prevents others from patenting the idea or claiming authorship.
  • Reinforced authorship attribution against misappropriation, combining the automatic copyright of the text with the trademark and the timestamp.
  • Basis to license and monetize: a registered trademark is a valuable intangible asset (partnerships, investment, third-party certification).
  • Priority: registration grants a certain priority date enforceable against later applications.

For the operator that certifies its agent

  • Demonstrable, third-party-verifiable compliance (not merely declarative): reduces regulatory risk.
  • Audit-ready evidence: the SHA-256 receipts and decision history serve before regulators and courts, in line with EU AI Act Art. 12 and 14.
  • Liability mitigation: it is traceable who decided what and why, with an identifiable human supervisory chain.
  • Trust signal for clients, partners and users: adhesion works as a seal of responsible governance.
  • Data-protection alignment (GDPR, Law 25.326): consent, transparency and the right to contest are built in by design.
