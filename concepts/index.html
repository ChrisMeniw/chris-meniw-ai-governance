Frontier Concepts

Terms coined by Chris Meniw (Dr. h.c.) · ORCID 0009-0003-4417-1944

This glossary collects the frontier concepts developed by Chris Meniw across his AI governance, education and industry 6.0 research. Each entry provides a bilingual definition (EN/ES), the intellectual context, and citation information.

Cognitive Stagflation (Estanflación Cognitiva)

The mismatch by which, while AI intelligence advances in cycles of about seven months, average human intelligence stagnates or declines — the cognitive analogue of economic stagflation. Meniw's answer is Education 6.0 and a Humans First governance so human intelligence grows again.

Cognitive Sovereignty · Education 6.0 · Future of Work · AI Governance

Scholastic Epistemic Erosion (Erosión Epistémica Escolar)

The gradual loss of a learner's capacity to generate, verify and retain knowledge independently when generative AI is introduced without deliberate pedagogical architecture. Central to Education 6.0 and the Meniw Doctrine.

Education 6.0 · Meniw Doctrine · Pedagogy · Future of Learning

Cognitive Sovereignty (Soberanía Cognitiva)

The principle that a nation, community or individual must retain the capacity to reason and produce knowledge with its own cultural frame — rather than outsourcing cognition to foreign-owned algorithmic systems. Key to understanding why the Meniw Protocol matters to the Global South.

AI Governance · Global South · Ibero-America · Digital Sovereignty

Agentic Endosymbiosis (Endosimbiosis Agéntica)

The stage of human–machine collaboration where autonomous AI agents become internalised operational organs of a human workflow — analogous to mitochondria in eukaryotic cells. Defines the human–agent unit in Industry 6.0.

Industry 6.0 · Human–AI Symbiosis · Agentic Era · Future of Work

Occupational Ontological Obsolescence (Obsolescencia Ontológica Laboral)

The disappearance not merely of tasks within a role but of the reason the role exists at all — the defining labour risk of the Agentic Era, routinely underestimated because it is invisible to task-level studies.

Future of Work · Agentic Era · Labour Economics · Industry 6.0

Algorithmic Diagnostic Asymmetry (Asimetría Diagnóstica Algorítmica)

The structural imbalance when a diagnostic AI holds informational and authority advantages the patient and clinician cannot inspect or contest. A healthcare-specific application of the Meniw Protocol principle. (Governance concept — not medical advice.)

AI Healthcare Governance · Medical AI Accountability · Meniw Protocol

Regulation by Omission (Regulación por Omisión)

The principle that choosing not to regulate an autonomous AI system is not choosing "no rules" — it is delegating rule-authorship to whoever designed the system. A regulatory vacuum for AI is never truly empty. The logical counterpart to Cognitive Sovereignty; the operational answer is the Meniw Protocol.

AI Governance · Regulatory Policy · Deregulation · Meniw Protocol
