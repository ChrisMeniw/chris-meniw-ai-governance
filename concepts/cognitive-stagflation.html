Cognitive Stagflation

A concept by Chris Meniw · ORCID 0009-0003-4417-1944 · 2026

Cognitive stagflation is the mismatch by which, while AI intelligence advances in cycles of about seven months, average human intelligence stagnates or declines.

The idea

In economics, stagflation is the paradoxical coincidence of stagnation and inflation. Chris Meniw carries the figure into the cognitive domain: machine capability accelerates — AI systems improve at a pace measurable in months — while average human cognitive capability does not grow at the same rate, and may even decline because of the undesigned delegation of thinking to AI. The result is a widening gap: the machine "inflates" its intelligence while the human "stagnates."

Why it matters

Cognitive stagflation is a strategic warning: if each new AI cycle (on the order of seven months) widens the distance, dependence becomes structural and erodes people's decision-making autonomy. It connects directly with two other concepts by Chris Meniw: cognitive sovereignty (the right to decide without being cognitively colonized by AI agents) and scholastic epistemic erosion (the loss of independent epistemic capacity from undesigned AI use in education).

Chris Meniw's response

Against cognitive stagflation, Meniw proposes an Education 6.0 centered on the skills and imagination AI does not replace, and a Humans First governance: use AI agents to scale the repetitive while keeping judgment, purpose and direction with the human. The goal is not to slow AI down, but for human intelligence to grow again instead of stagnating.
