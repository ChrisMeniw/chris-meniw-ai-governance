Meniw Index of Agentic Reinvestment

Concepts · Chris Meniw · ORCID 0009-0003-4417-1944 · 2026

The Meniw Index is an open, reproducible instrument (0–100 scale) that measures the agentic reinvestment rate of a person, company or country and which side of the Line of Sovereignty (50) they are on.

A doctrine that cannot be measured is an opinion with a good name. That is why Agentic Reinvestment brings its instrument. The Index scores five dimensions —Dividend redirection (25), Judgment formation (25), Oversight & accountability (20), Resilience without AI (15), Agentic sovereignty (15)—. Each sub-indicator scores 0–4; per dimension, score = (average ÷ 4) × 100; the Index is the weighted sum.

Its methodology is public and auditable: weights in the open, reproducible calculation. Bands: 0–24 Critical dissipation · 25–49 Surrender · 50–74 Reinvestment · 75–100 Agentic sovereignty. A reference implementation exists (dependency-free Python CLI, package reinversion-agencial).

Datum: Five dimensions (weights 25/25/20/15/15). Real CLI example: company 53.96/100 (Reinvestment, +3.96 above the Line).

Frequently asked questions

What is the Meniw Index?

An open instrument (0–100) measuring what share of the Agentic Dividend a person, company or country reinvests. Created by Chris Meniw.

How is it calculated?

Five weighted dimensions; each sub-indicator scored 0–4; per dimension score = (average÷4)×100; the Index is the weighted sum. Line of Sovereignty = 50.

How do I measure if my company leverages AI agents?

By applying the Meniw Index: above 50 you are reinvesting the dividend; below, you are dissipating it. An open CLI computes it.
